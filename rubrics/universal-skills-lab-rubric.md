# CIS111 Universal Skills Lab Rubric

**Status:** Canonical for CIS111 Skills Labs (Modules 1 through 11).
**Date:** 2026-05-17
**Source:** Designed in a prior Claude session, applied to Canvas M1 Skills Lab 1A (assignment 18504659, rubric id 2801576) on `pc.dev.cis111.vega` (course 1437418).
**Total:** 40 pts. Four criteria. Four levels (Mastery 10 / Proficiency 7 / Developing 4 / Emerging 1).

This rubric is **framework-agnostic and scenario-agnostic.** The descriptors apply to any Skills Lab in any module where students identify stakeholders, apply ethical frameworks, recommend a course of action, and reflect on professional responsibility.

---

## The Rubric

| Criteria | Mastery (10) | Proficiency (7) | Developing (4) | Emerging (1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Foundation Analysis**<br>*Identifying stakeholders, ethical issues, and key facts in the scenario.* | Identifies all major stakeholders with specific descriptions of their interests and what each stands to gain or lose. Names ethical tensions precisely, identifying the values or rights in conflict. Selects key facts from the scenario and explains why each matters for ethical analysis. | Identifies most stakeholders with reasonable descriptions of their interests. Names ethical issues with some specificity. Selects key facts but explanation of their ethical relevance is light. | Identifies some stakeholders but descriptions miss key interests or remain at the surface. Ethical issues are vague or generic. Key facts are listed without clear connection to ethical analysis. | Lists stakeholders without meaningful description. Ethical issues are absent or off-topic. Key facts are missing or unrelated to the ethical question. |
| **Ethical Framework Application**<br>*Applying ethical frameworks to the scenario with internal logic and specific evidence.* | Applies each required framework accurately with clear internal reasoning. References specific scenario details to support the analysis. Shows how each framework produces a distinct recommendation or conclusion. Demonstrates the framework's logic, not just its label. | Applies each required framework correctly with adequate reasoning. Includes some scenario-specific references. Conclusions follow from the framework but reasoning is less developed. | Applies frameworks with gaps in reasoning or limited connection to the scenario. May confuse one framework's logic with another. Conclusions are stated without showing how the framework produced them. | Attempts framework application but with significant errors. Frameworks are named without being meaningfully applied. |
| **Reasoning, Recommendation & Reflection**<br>*Synthesizing the analysis into a defensible recommendation and reflecting on professional responsibility.* | Recommendation is clear, specific, and supported by at least two frameworks with explicit reasoning. Addresses counterarguments or competing interests. Professional reflection takes a clear position, identifies a specific obligation, and ties it to a named framework. Responses show ethical reasoning, not just opinion. | Recommendation is clear and supported by at least one framework with reasonable justification. Reflection takes a position and references a framework, though the connection is less developed. | Recommendation is present but weakly supported or lacks clear connection to ethical reasoning. Reflection is brief or unclear about the obligation or the framework behind it. | Recommendation is vague, generic, or unsupported by ethical reasoning. Reflection is missing or disconnected from the scenario. |
| **Documentation & Communication**<br>*Writing quality, organization, terminology, and length compliance.* | Writing is clear and organized by part with appropriate headings. Ethical terminology is used accurately throughout. Reasoning flows logically between sections. Meets the assignment word count. A colleague could read and build on this analysis. | Writing is clear with mostly accurate terminology and reasonable organization. Meets word count. Minor mechanics issues that do not interfere with meaning. | Writing is understandable but disorganized or imprecise with terminology. May fall short on word count. Mechanics issues affect clarity in places. | Writing is unclear, contains frequent errors, lacks professional tone, or falls well below word count. |

---

## Why this rubric is universal

* **No scenario-specific language.** Descriptors apply to any case (CrimeMap, employee monitoring, AI hiring tool, healthcare AI, IP dispute, security breach, etc.) without rewriting.
* **Framework-agnostic.** "Each required framework" works whether the module covers utilitarianism + deontology, virtue ethics + social contract theory, or any combination called out in that module's Skills Lab instructions.
* **Question-agnostic.** "Recommendation" and "professional reflection" describe the type of work rather than locking into a specific question wording.
* **Feedback-ready.** Each descriptor reads as a paste-ready SpeedGrader comment.

## Effort-to-points calibration

| Assignment | Time | Points | Min/point |
|---|---|---|---|
| Skills Lab | 90-120 min | 40 | 2.6-3.0 |
| Question & Discuss | 30-50 min | 16 | 1.9-3.1 |

Ratios match, signaling that both assignments matter and effort gets rewarded consistently. Skills Lab carries 2.5x the points of the discussion because the lab is where deep framework application happens; the discussion builds on that foundation.

## M12 Capstone variant

Module 12 (the capstone) uses a variant of this rubric. Two criteria are renamed to reflect the synthesis scope of the capstone:

* **Foundation Analysis** (unchanged)
* **Ethical Framework Application** (unchanged)
* **Course Concept Synthesis** (replaces Reasoning, Recommendation & Reflection)
* **Writing Quality and Professional Reflection** (replaces Documentation & Communication)

See `modules/12-professional-ethics-and-capstone/module-12.md` for the M12 capstone-variant descriptors.
