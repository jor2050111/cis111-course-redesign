# Module 3: Social Media and Privacy

Every scroll, click, and share generates data. Social media platforms have become central to how people communicate, consume news, and form communities, but they also raise profound ethical questions about privacy, consent, and the power that platform companies hold over public discourse.

This module examines the economic, legal, ethical, and privacy dimensions of social media. You will evaluate how platforms collect and monetize user data, how algorithmic systems shape what people see, and what ethical obligations platform companies owe to their users and to society.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** Modules 1-2
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO IV

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **3.1-LO1 (Analyze):** Analyze how social media platforms balance economic incentives with ethical obligations to users (CLO IV)
* **3.2-LO2 (Evaluate):** Evaluate the ethical implications of data collection practices and algorithmic content curation (CLO IV)
* **3.3-LO3 (Apply):** Apply ethical reasoning to assess privacy and consent issues on social media platforms (CLO IV)
