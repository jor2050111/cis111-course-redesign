# Module 1: Ethics and Ethical Theories

Every day, technology professionals face decisions that affect millions of users, from how data is collected to how algorithms make recommendations. But how do we determine what is "right" when reasonable people disagree? This module introduces the foundational ethical theories that provide structured approaches to answering that question.

You will explore four major ethical frameworks: utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics, and social contract theory. By the end of this module, you will have a reasoning toolkit that you can apply throughout the rest of this course and throughout your IT career.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** None
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab reflection
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO I

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **1.1-LO1 (Understand):** Explain the core principles of utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics, and social contract theory (CLO I)
* **1.2-LO2 (Analyze):** Compare the strengths and limitations of each ethical theory when applied to technology scenarios (CLO I)
* **1.3-LO3 (Apply):** Apply an ethical framework to a real-world IT decision-making scenario (CLO I)
