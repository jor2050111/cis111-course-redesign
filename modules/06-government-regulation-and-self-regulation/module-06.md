# Module 6: Government Regulation and Self-Regulation

Technology evolves faster than the laws designed to govern it. Legislators work to protect citizens from data misuse, privacy violations, and unsafe practices, while industry professionals argue that self-regulation can be more responsive and effective. This ongoing tension shapes the regulatory landscape that IT professionals must understand and operate within.

This module examines major government regulations affecting the technology sector, including GDPR, CCPA, COPPA, and HIPAA. You will also evaluate the ethical challenges unique to healthcare IT environments, where mobile and wireless technologies introduce specific risks to patient privacy and safety. Finally, you will analyze the role of professional self-regulation and industry standards.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 5-6 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** Modules 1-5
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO VI, CLO X

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **6.1-LO1 (Analyze):** Analyze the ethical challenges of using mobile and wireless technologies in healthcare environments (CLO VI)
* **6.2-LO2 (Evaluate):** Evaluate the effectiveness of key government regulations (GDPR, CCPA, COPPA, HIPAA) in protecting individual rights (CLO X)
* **6.3-LO3 (Compare):** Compare the strengths and limitations of government regulation versus professional self-regulation in IT ethics (CLO X)
