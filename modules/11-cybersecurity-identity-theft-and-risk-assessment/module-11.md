# Module 11: Identity Theft and Cybercrime

Identity theft remains one of the fastest-growing crimes in the digital age, affecting millions of people each year. Behind the statistics are real people who face financial ruin, damaged credit, and months or years of recovery. For IT professionals, understanding the methods, impacts, and prevention strategies for identity theft is both a professional responsibility and an ethical imperative.

This module examines the major forms of identity theft, the broader cybercrime landscape, and the ethical obligations of IT professionals to protect the individuals and organizations they serve. You will analyze how identity theft occurs, evaluate prevention strategies, and consider the human cost of these crimes.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **11.1-LO1 (Analyze):** Analyze the major methods used to commit identity theft and the vulnerabilities they exploit (CLO XIV)

* **11.3-LO3 (Assess):** Assess the impact of identity theft and cybercrime on victims, organizations, and society (CLO XIV)
