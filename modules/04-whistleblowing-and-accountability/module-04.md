# Module 4: Whistleblowing and Accountability

What do you do when you discover your employer is engaging in practices that are unethical or illegal? Whistleblowing is one of the most personally consequential ethical decisions a professional can face, carrying significant risk alongside the potential for significant public benefit.

This module examines the legal protections available to whistleblowers, the fiduciary responsibilities organizations hold over private information, and the ethical tension between loyalty to an employer and accountability to the public. You will analyze landmark whistleblowing cases in the technology sector and evaluate the frameworks that govern these difficult choices.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** Modules 1-3
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO V, CLO VII

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **4.1-LO1 (Understand):** Describe the legal protections available to whistleblowers, including Sarbanes-Oxley provisions (CLO V)
* **4.2-LO2 (Analyze):** Analyze the ethical tension between organizational loyalty and the obligation to report wrongdoing (CLO V)
* **4.3-LO3 (Evaluate):** Evaluate fiduciary responsibilities regarding the protection and handling of private information (CLO VII)
