# Module 2: Corporate Responsibility

When a tech company prioritizes growth over user safety, who bears the responsibility? When a product manager is told to ship a feature they believe is harmful, what should they do? This module examines the ethical landscape where personal values, professional obligations, and corporate interests intersect.

You will analyze the distinction between professional and personal ethics, explore shareholder versus stakeholder theory, and evaluate how corporate governance structures influence ethical outcomes. These concepts are central to understanding how organizations in the technology sector approach (or avoid) their responsibilities.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** Module 1
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO II, CLO III

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **2.1-LO1 (Analyze):** Distinguish between personal ethics, professional ethics, and organizational ethics in IT contexts (CLO II)
* **2.2-LO2 (Evaluate):** Evaluate the competing obligations of shareholders and stakeholders in technology companies (CLO III)
* **2.3-LO3 (Analyze):** Analyze how corporate governance structures influence ethical decision-making and corporate social responsibility (CLO III)
