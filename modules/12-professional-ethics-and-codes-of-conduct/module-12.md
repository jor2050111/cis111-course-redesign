# Module 12: Professional Ethics and Codes of Conduct

Throughout this course, you have built an ethical reasoning toolkit and applied it to a wide range of IT challenges, from corporate responsibility to cybersecurity. In this final module, you will examine the formal codes of ethics that guide IT professionals and bring together everything you have learned into a capstone analysis.

Professional codes of ethics, such as those published by ACM, IEEE, and (ISC)2, provide concrete guidance for practitioners. But how well do these codes address the ethical challenges of emerging technologies like artificial intelligence, the Internet of Things, and autonomous systems? You will critique these codes and evaluate their relevance to the ethical landscape that today's IT professionals face.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 5-6 hours (reading + activities + capstone)
* **Prerequisites:** Modules 1-11
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Capstone analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO XVII

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **12.1-LO1 (Analyze):** Analyze the key principles and provisions of major IT professional codes of ethics (ACM, IEEE, (ISC)2) (CLO XVII)
* **12.2-LO2 (Evaluate):** Critique professional codes of ethics by evaluating their applicability to emerging technology challenges (CLO XVII)
* **12.3-LO3 (Create):** Synthesize course concepts into a comprehensive ethical analysis of a current technology issue (CLO XVII)
