# Module 8: Freedom of Expression and Defamation

The internet has given billions of people a platform to speak, but it has also amplified the reach of harmful speech. Where should the line be drawn between protecting free expression and preventing real harm? This question is at the center of some of the most heated debates in technology ethics today.

This module examines the First Amendment and its application to digital speech, how hate speech is classified and addressed, platform responsibility for user-generated content, and the legal and ethical dimensions of online defamation. You will classify different categories of harmful speech and evaluate the competing interests of free expression and public safety.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** Modules 1-7
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO XII

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **8.1-LO1 (Understand):** Describe the scope and limits of First Amendment protections as they apply to digital speech (CLO XII)
* **8.2-LO2 (Classify):** Classify different categories of harmful online speech, including hate speech and defamation (CLO XII)
* **8.3-LO3 (Evaluate):** Evaluate the ethical responsibilities of platforms in moderating user-generated content (CLO XII)
