# Module 7: Intellectual Property and Fair Use

Who owns a piece of software? Can you use a copyrighted image in a class presentation? Is it ethical to reverse-engineer a competitor's product? Intellectual property law exists to balance the rights of creators with the public's interest in access and innovation, but applying these principles in the digital world is rarely straightforward.

This module examines the four pillars of intellectual property (copyright, patents, trademarks, and trade secrets), the fair use doctrine, and the ethical considerations surrounding open source licensing. You will interpret how these legal frameworks apply to common IT scenarios and evaluate the tensions between protecting creators and promoting access to knowledge.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** Modules 1-6
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO XI

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **7.1-LO1 (Understand):** Explain the key forms of intellectual property protection: copyright, patents, trademarks, and trade secrets (CLO XI)
* **7.2-LO2 (Apply):** Apply the four-factor fair use test to determine whether a use of copyrighted material is legally and ethically defensible (CLO XI)
* **7.3-LO3 (Evaluate):** Evaluate the ethical implications of open source licensing and digital piracy in the technology industry (CLO XI)
