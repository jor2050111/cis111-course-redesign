# Module 9: Privacy and Workplace Monitoring

Your employer can likely see your emails, track your keystrokes, monitor your web browsing, and even use your laptop's camera. As remote work has expanded, so has the reach of workplace surveillance technologies. But where is the ethical line between legitimate security interests and invasive monitoring?

This module examines contemporary issues related to workplace monitoring, including the technologies used for employee surveillance, the legal and ethical boundaries of these practices, and how organizations can balance their security needs with employees' reasonable expectations of privacy. You will assess the ethical dimensions of monitoring from the perspectives of both employers and employees.

## Module Overview 🧭

* **Estimated time:** 4-5 hours (reading + activities + discussion)
* **Prerequisites:** Modules 1-8
* **Deliverables:** Discussion post, Quick Checks, Skills Lab analysis
* **CLOs addressed:** CLO XIII

## Learning Objectives 🎯

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

* **9.1-LO1 (Understand):** Describe the major types of workplace monitoring technologies and their capabilities (CLO XIII)
* **9.2-LO2 (Assess):** Assess the ethical arguments for and against common employee surveillance practices (CLO XIII)
* **9.3-LO3 (Evaluate):** Evaluate workplace monitoring policies for their balance of organizational security and employee privacy (CLO XIII)
