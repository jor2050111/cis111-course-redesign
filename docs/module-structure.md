# CIS111 Course Redesign - Module Structure

**Course:** CIS111 - Ethics in Information Technology
**Structure:** 12 modules organized into 4 thematic parts
**Schedule:** 9-week accelerated term (or adaptable to 16-week)

---

## Rationale for 12-Module Structure

**Why 12 modules:**
* Mirrors the successful CIS215 textbook chapter structure
* Maps cleanly to the 18 CLOs (1-2 CLOs per module)
* Provides enough granularity for focused weekly topics
* Adaptable to both 9-week (doubled up) and 16-week (one per week + exams) formats
* Each module covers a cohesive ethical theme with real-world applications

**What each module contains:**
* Module Learning Objectives (MLOs) aligned to specific CLOs
* Instructional content (readings, case studies, multimedia)
* Discussion prompts or ethical scenario activities
* Assessment (quiz, reflection, case analysis, or skills lab)

---

## Part I: Foundations of Ethics (Modules 1-3)

**Theme:** Building an ethical reasoning toolkit

**Learning arc:** Ethical Theories → Business Ethics → Corporate Responsibility

### Module 1: Ethics and Ethical Theories
**Focus:** Core ethical frameworks and their application to IT
* Utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics, social contract theory
* How ethical theories apply to technology decisions
* Building an ethical reasoning framework

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO I: Compare a variety of different ethical theories

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section I (Ethics and Ethical Theories)

---

### Module 2: Professional Business Ethics
**Focus:** Ethical judgment in professional IT contexts
* Professional ethics vs. personal ethics
* Complexities of ethical decision-making in business
* Ethical dilemmas in technology workplaces

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO II: Analyze professional business ethics and the complexities of making ethical judgments

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section I.b, I.c

---

### Module 3: Corporate Governance and Stakeholder Responsibility
**Focus:** Corporate ethics, ownership, and accountability
* Responsibilities to shareholders vs. stakeholders
* Corporate governance structures
* Corporate social responsibility in tech companies

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO III: Examine issues relating to corporate ownership, corporate governance, and responsiveness to corporate stakeholders

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section II.a, II.b

---

**Part I Milestone:** Students can identify, compare, and apply ethical theories to IT business scenarios. Students can distinguish between shareholder and stakeholder perspectives.

---

## Part II: Digital Rights and Responsibilities (Modules 4-6)

**Theme:** Individual and organizational accountability in a connected world

**Learning arc:** Social Media → Whistleblowing → Healthcare Privacy

### Module 4: Social Media Responsibility
**Focus:** Economic, legal, ethical, and privacy dimensions of social media
* Social media platforms and ethical obligations
* Data collection, targeted advertising, and consent
* Content moderation and algorithmic bias
* User privacy vs. business models

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO IV: Evaluate how social media responsibility encompasses economic, legal, ethical, and privacy issues

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section II.c

---

### Module 5: Whistleblowing and Accountability
**Focus:** Whistleblower protections, fiduciary duty, and private information
* When and how whistleblowing is justified
* Legal protections (Sarbanes-Oxley, Dodd-Frank)
* Fiduciary responsibilities regarding private data
* Balancing loyalty and ethical obligation

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO V: Illustrate whistle-blowing and protection for whistle-blowers
* CLO VII: Interpret fiduciary responsibilities of private information

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section II.e, II.f

---

### Module 6: Healthcare IT Ethics
**Focus:** Ethical challenges in healthcare technology
* HIPAA and health data privacy
* Mobile health (mHealth) and wireless technology risks
* Telemedicine ethics
* Balancing innovation with patient privacy

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO VI: Analyze the major ethical challenges of operating in a healthcare environment using mobile and wireless technologies

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section II.d

---

**Part II Milestone:** Students can evaluate real-world scenarios involving social media ethics, whistleblower protections, and healthcare data privacy. Students understand fiduciary responsibilities.

---

## Part III: Legal Frameworks and IT Obligations (Modules 7-9)

**Theme:** Where law meets technology ethics

**Learning arc:** Software Liability → Government Regulation → Intellectual Property

### Module 7: Software Liability and Secure Coding
**Focus:** Responsibility for software quality and security
* Software defects and liability frameworks
* Ethical obligations in software development
* Secure coding principles and cost/benefit analysis
* When software failures cause harm

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO VIII: Examine issues with software and software liability
* CLO IX: Apply cost/benefit analysis in information technology ethical decision-making

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section III

---

### Module 8: Government Regulation and Self-Regulation
**Focus:** The role of law and professional standards in IT ethics
* Key IT regulations (GDPR, CCPA, COPPA, etc.)
* Professional self-regulation and sanctions
* Corporate social audits
* Balancing innovation with regulation

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO X: Evaluate the influence of government regulations as well as professional self-regulation and sanctions in ethical information technology decision-making

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section IV.a

---

### Module 9: Intellectual Property and Fair Use
**Focus:** Protecting and respecting creative and digital works
* Copyright, patents, trademarks, trade secrets
* Fair use doctrine and its limits
* Open source licensing
* Digital piracy and ethical considerations

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO XI: Interpret intellectual property, copyright laws, and fair use doctrine

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section IV.b

---

**Part III Milestone:** Students can analyze software liability scenarios, evaluate regulatory frameworks, and interpret intellectual property protections.

---

## Part IV: Security, Privacy, and Professional Practice (Modules 10-12)

**Theme:** Protecting people and systems, becoming ethical professionals

**Learning arc:** Freedom vs. Harm → Security & Crime → Professional Codes

### Module 10: Freedom of Expression, Hate Speech, and Workplace Privacy
**Focus:** Balancing rights with protections
* First Amendment and digital speech
* Hate speech classification and platform responsibility
* Defamation in online contexts
* Employee monitoring and surveillance ethics

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO XII: Classify hate speech and defamation
* CLO XIII: Assess contemporary issues related to workplace monitoring

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section V.a, V.b

---

### Module 11: Cybersecurity, Identity Theft, and Risk Assessment
**Focus:** Security threats, breaches, and organizational response
* Types of security breaches and their impact
* Identity theft: methods, prevention, and response
* Risk assessment frameworks
* Formulating security policies
* Prevention, detection, and incident response

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO XIV: Analyze the major identity theft issues
* CLO XV: Evaluate Information Technology security breaches and risk assessments
* CLO XVI: Formulate security policies within Information Technology
* CLO XVIII: Develop prevention, detection, and responses to Information Technology security attacks

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section V.c, V.d, V.e, V.f

---

### Module 12: Professional Codes of Ethics and Capstone
**Focus:** Becoming an ethical IT professional
* ACM Code of Ethics, IEEE Code of Ethics, (ISC)² Code of Ethics
* Comparing codes across IT disciplines
* Applying ethical frameworks to emerging technologies (AI, IoT, etc.)
* Capstone: Integrating course concepts into an ethical analysis

**CLO Alignment:**
* CLO XVII: Critique professional codes of ethics for Information Technology professionals

**Course Outline Alignment:** Section V.g

---

**Part IV Milestone:** Students can classify harmful speech, assess cybersecurity risks, formulate security policies, and critique professional codes of ethics. Students synthesize course concepts through capstone analysis.

## Bloom's Taxonomy Progression Across Parts

### Part I (Modules 1-3): Remember, Understand
**Focus:** Building foundational knowledge of ethical theories and corporate structures
* Define and compare ethical theories
* Explain professional ethics concepts
* Describe corporate governance structures

### Part II (Modules 4-6): Understand, Apply, Analyze
**Focus:** Applying ethical reasoning to specific domains
* Evaluate social media ethical dilemmas
* Analyze whistleblowing scenarios
* Apply ethical frameworks to healthcare IT cases

### Part III (Modules 7-9): Apply, Analyze, Evaluate
**Focus:** Legal and regulatory analysis
* Apply cost/benefit analysis to IT decisions
* Evaluate regulatory approaches
* Interpret intellectual property frameworks

### Part IV (Modules 10-12): Analyze, Evaluate, Create
**Focus:** Synthesis and professional practice
* Classify harmful content and assess privacy issues
* Formulate security policies
* Critique professional codes of ethics
* Develop comprehensive ethical analyses (capstone)
